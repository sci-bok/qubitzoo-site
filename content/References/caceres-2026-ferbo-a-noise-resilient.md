The FerBo qubit is a proposed superconducting circuit that hybridizes a high-impedance fluxonium-like bosonic mode with the fermionic Andreev degree of freedom of a highly transmissive weak link. In the protected regime, the lowest two qubit states live predominantly in different Andreev manifolds, suppressing charge-mediated relaxation while phase delocalization suppresses flux-noise dephasing.

## Abstract
We propose a novel superconducting quantum circuit that should be robust against both relaxation and dephasing over a wide and experimentally accessible parameter range. The circuit consists of a parallel arrangement of a large inductance, a small capacitor, and a well-transmitting Josephson weak link. Protection against relaxation arises from the hybridization between the fermionic degree of freedom associated with Andreev levels in the weak link and the bosonic electromagnetic mode of the LC circuit, hence its name: FerBo. Furthermore, as in the fluxonium qubit, delocalization of the wavefunctions in phase space provides resilience against dephasing.

## Key Findings

- Proposes a dual-protected superconducting qubit using only one bosonic mode plus an Andreev weak-link degree of freedom.
- Identifies a protected regime with $Z/R_Q \gg 1$ and $\epsilon_r/E_C \ll 1$, separated by a sharp crossover near $Z/R_Q \approx 2E_C/(\pi\epsilon_r)$.
- Shows that deep in the protected regime, $|0\rangle$ lives mainly in the $|-\rangle$ Andreev manifold while $|1\rangle$ lives mainly in the $|+\rangle$ manifold.
- Predicts roughly four orders of magnitude suppression of the charge-relaxation matrix element at the zero-flux sweet spot.
- Remains a 2026 arXiv-only proposal, with no later experimental FerBo-specific realization found during this audit.
