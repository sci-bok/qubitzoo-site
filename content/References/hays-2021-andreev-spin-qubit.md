# Coherent Manipulation of an Andreev Spin Qubit

**Hays et al., Science 373, 430 (2021)** — [arXiv:2008.02348](https://arxiv.org/abs/2008.02348)

## Summary

Demonstrates coherent control of a qubit encoded in the spin degree of freedom of an Andreev bound state in a semiconductor-superconductor nanowire Josephson junction. The qubit transitions are driven by microwave radiation and read out via the state-dependent supercurrent (dispersive readout through a coupled resonator).

## Key Physics

- **Andreev bound states**: Subgap states in a Josephson junction that carry supercurrent. In a nanowire junction with spin-orbit coupling, these states have spin character.
- **Qubit encoding**: Spin-split Andreev levels $|\uparrow\rangle$ and $|\downarrow\rangle$ form the computational basis.
- **Spin-orbit coupling**: Essential for enabling microwave-driven spin transitions (electric dipole spin resonance).
- **Readout**: Different spin states carry different supercurrents → dispersive shift of coupled resonator frequency.

## Significance

Bridges superconducting and spin qubit worlds — a semiconductor-based qubit that is read out like a superconducting qubit. Potential for topological protection if combined with Majorana physics.
