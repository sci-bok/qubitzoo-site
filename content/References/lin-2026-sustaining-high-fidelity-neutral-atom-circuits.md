Shows that neutral-atom logic can remain near threshold fidelity across repeated circuit rounds by combining erasure detection with hardware-efficient mid-circuit operations.

## Key Results
- Reports a raw two-qubit controlled-gate fidelity of 99.60(1)% and 99.81(1)% with non-destructive erasure detection.
- Uses mid-circuit Raman sideband cooling and qubit re-initialization to sustain ~99.8% performance across repeated rounds.
- Directly addresses cumulative motional heating and atom-loss degradation in deep neutral-atom circuits.
- Strengthens the case for neutral atoms as a fault-tolerant architecture rather than only a shallow-circuit demonstrator.
