## Citation

D. A. Rower, L. Ding, H. Zhang, M. Hays, J. An, P. M. Harrington, I. T. Rosen, J. M. Gertler, L. B. Nguyen, K. Serniak, T. M. Hoang, M. E. Schwartz, W. D. Oliver, and S. Gustavsson, "Suppressing Counter-Rotating Errors for Fast Single-Qubit Gates with Fluxonium," [PRX Quantum **5**, 040342 (2024)](https://doi.org/10.1103/PRXQuantum.5.040342) · [arXiv:2406.08295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.08295)

## Key Results

- Achieved **99.998% single-qubit gate fidelity** on a fluxonium qubit — the highest for any superconducting qubit.
- Introduced circularly polarized microwave drives (simultaneous charge and flux drives) to suppress counter-rotating errors inherent to low-frequency qubits.
- Developed "commensurate pulses" — precisely timed pulses that regularize periodic counter-rotating errors without extra calibration.
- Demonstrated that these techniques are platform-agnostic and applicable to other qubit architectures.
