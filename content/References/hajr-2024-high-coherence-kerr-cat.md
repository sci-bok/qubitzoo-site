Shows that Kerr-cat qubits can be implemented in a scalable planar superconducting circuit without sacrificing high coherence. The work combines an on-chip band-block filter, high-fidelity QND readout, and universal-control primitives in a 2D architecture.

## Key Results
- 99.6% quantum non-demolition readout fidelity for a cat with eight photons
- Bit-flip time above 1 ms for cat sizes up to ten photons
- On-chip filter provides about 30 dB isolation at the qubit frequency while preserving stabilization and readout access
- Demonstrates an X(pi/2) gate via phase modulation of the stabilization drive in a scalable 2D circuit
