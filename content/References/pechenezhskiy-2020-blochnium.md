# The Superconducting Quasicharge Qubit

**Pechenezhskiy et al., Nature 585, 368 (2020)** — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2687-9)

## Summary

Demonstrates the blochnium — a superconducting qubit operating in the regime where the inductive energy $E_L$ is so small that the phase variable $\varphi$ becomes quasi-continuous and the conjugate "quasicharge" $p$ (Bloch momentum) becomes the good quantum number. This is the dual of the transmon: where the transmon makes charge continuous by increasing $E_J/E_C$, blochnium makes flux/phase continuous by decreasing $E_L$.

## Key Physics

- Operates in the regime $E_L \ll E_C, E_J$ (extreme fluxonium limit)
- The qubit transition is between states in the lowest Bloch band of the Josephson cosine potential
- "Quasicharge" $p$ is analogous to crystal momentum in solid-state physics
- Exponentially insensitive to both charge noise AND flux noise (in certain parameter regimes)

## Duality with Transmon

| Property | Transmon | Blochnium |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Regime | $E_J \gg E_C$ | $E_L \ll E_C, E_J$ |
| Good quantum number | Phase $\varphi$ | Quasicharge $p$ |
| Charge sensitivity | Exponentially suppressed | Exactly zero |
| Flux sensitivity | Strong | Can be suppressed |
