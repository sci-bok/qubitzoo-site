Demonstration of a 256-atom programmable quantum simulator using ⁸⁷Rb optical tweezer arrays, exploring quantum phases of matter at unprecedented scale for neutral atom platforms.

## Key Results
- Realized a programmable quantum simulator with 256 individually trapped ⁸⁷Rb atoms in reconfigurable tweezer arrays
- Demonstrated exploration of quantum phases of matter including observation of a quantum phase transition
- Implemented optimization algorithms leveraging the large atom number
- Established the path toward large-scale neutral atom quantum computing and simulation
