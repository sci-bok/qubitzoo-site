Constructs the first asymptotically good quantum LDPC codes: families of $[[n, k, d]]$ codes with $k = \Theta(n)$ and $d = \Theta(n)$ while maintaining constant stabilizer weight. Resolves a 25-year-old open problem in quantum error correction and opens the path to constant-overhead fault tolerance.

## Key Results
- First quantum codes with linear rate AND linear distance AND constant check weight
- Encoding rate: k/n → constant as n → ∞
- Distance: d = Θ(n) (optimal scaling)
- Based on balanced product of classical LDPC codes over suitable groups
