## Summary

Proposes the quarton — a superconducting circuit element with purely quartic ($\varphi^4$) potential and no quadratic ($\varphi^2$) term — for engineering ultrastrong nonlinear coupling between linearly decoupled transmon qubits. Achieves gigahertz-scale cross-Kerr interaction ($\chi_{zz}$), an order of magnitude stronger than conventional dispersive coupling. The circuit uses balanced series and lone Josephson junctions with tilt parameter $2\alpha \approx 1$. Applications include ultrafast entangling gates and single photon detection.
