# Coherent Manipulation of Electronic States in a Double Quantum Dot

**Hayashi et al., PRL 91, 226804 (2003)** — [arXiv:cond-mat/0308362](https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/0308362)

## Summary

Demonstrates coherent charge oscillations in a semiconductor double quantum dot — the charge-qubit analogue of the Nakamura experiment for superconducting circuits. An electron oscillates between left and right dots with a coherence time of ~1 ns, limited by charge noise from the solid-state environment.

## Key Physics

- Qubit states: $|L\rangle$ (electron in left dot) and $|R\rangle$ (electron in right dot)
- Tunnel coupling $t_c$ provides coherent mixing; detuning $\epsilon$ controlled by gate voltage
- At zero detuning: maximal tunneling, equal superposition
- Coherent oscillations observed via pulsed gate voltage + time-resolved charge detection
- $T_2^* \sim 1$ ns — much shorter than spin qubits due to charge noise sensitivity

## Significance

Demonstrated the feasibility and limitations of semiconductor charge qubits. The short coherence times motivated the field to pivot toward spin-based encodings ([[single-spin-quantum-dot-loss-divincenzo-qubit]], [[singlet-triplet-qubit]]) where spin is naturally decoupled from electric field fluctuations.
