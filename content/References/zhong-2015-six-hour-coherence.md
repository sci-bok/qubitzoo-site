## Summary

Demonstrates a coherence time of 6 hours for $^{151}\text{Eu}^{3+}$ nuclear spins in Y₂SiO₅ at 2 K, using a zero-first-order Zeeman (ZEFOZ) magnetic field and dynamical decoupling. Record solid-state coherence time by orders of magnitude. Decoherence rate measured at $8 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{s}^{-1}$. Establishes rare-earth nuclear spins as the longest-lived solid-state quantum memory.
