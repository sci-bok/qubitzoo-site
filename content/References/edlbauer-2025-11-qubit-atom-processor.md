## Citation

H. Edlbauer, J. Wang et al., "An 11-qubit atom processor in silicon," [Nature **648**, 569 (2025)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-09827-w) · [arXiv:2506.03567](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03567)

## Key Results

- 11-qubit processor with 9 nuclear spin qubits + 2 electron spin qubits using precision-placed phosphorus donors in ²⁸Si.
- Single-qubit gate fidelity up to **99.99%** (nuclear spin).
- Two-qubit CROT gate fidelity of **99.64%** (electron exchange).
- Scaling demonstrated without fidelity degradation.
- Built by Silicon Quantum Computing (SQC) using scanning tunnelling microscope (STM) lithography for atomic-precision donor placement.
