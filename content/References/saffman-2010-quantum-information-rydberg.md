Definitive review of Rydberg-mediated neutral-atom quantum information, covering blockade gates, collective encoding, error sources, and scaling limits.

## Key Results
- Synthesizes the blockade-gate mechanism that underpins modern neutral-atom entangling operations.
- Reviews neutral-atom qubit encodings, optical trapping, and the dominant laser-noise and motional-error channels.
- Establishes why identical atoms plus reconfigurable geometry make neutral-atom systems attractive for scalable quantum computing.
- Provides the canonical reference point for comparing later Rydberg-gate fidelity improvements.
